How to use Debt Manager App?

Welcome to the quick guide to using the Debt Manager app!

Below you can find detailed instructions on how to use it. In case of any confusion or questions, you can contact the developer by email: AkiraGoto15@gmail.com.

Main Menu

This is what the menu looks like:

There are four buttons here:

- Help (You are here now).

- Debts I owe.

- Debts They owe.

- Exit.

To record your debts, go to the "Debts I owe" section. Section "Debts They owe" has the same function, created for your convenience only ;)

Also, there is (Exit) button. Just press it, if you want close application.

Debts

After the "Add debt" button has been pressed, the following window will appear:

To enter a debt name, click on this field. You can use absolutely any characters in the debt name:

To enter a debt amount, click on amount field. Remember, that after saving, it will not be possible to change the amount of debt. To enter fractional amount, use (.) .

To save a debt, click the "Save" button, after which you will see a notification about saving. Remember that it is impossible to save a debt if its name or quantity is not entered, or both fields are left empty. If you forget to save a debt, then after exiting to the menu, the filled field will automatically disappear.

You can also delete a debt by clicking the "Delete" button if this debt is no longer relevant.

There is also a "More" button. When you click on it, a window with debt details will open. Here you can enter the amounts by which the debt should be reduced:

The debt details are filled in the same way as the debt itself. After clicking the "add" button, the payment will be automatically saved. To delete, you must select a specific payment and click "delete". The deleted payment will automatically be added back to the debt.

To add a payment, you must enter its amount in the text field, then select a date on the calendar. The default date is the current day.

You also have an initial and total debt amount. The initial displays the amount of the amount in the debt, the total is the amount that was paid. As a result of paying off the debt, the total amount will be equal to the original amount of the debt:
